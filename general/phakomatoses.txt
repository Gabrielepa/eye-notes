phakomatoses AKA neurooculocutaneous syndromes

  NF1
    1/2 AD
    1/2 sporadic
    melanocytic lesions:
      melanocytic lesions
        skin
          cafe au lait spots
        uveal tract
          Lisch nodules
            commonly visible at 5+ YO
            a few patients don't get them until their teens
          choroidal nodules
      glial cell lesions
        skin
          nodular neurofibromas
          plexiform neurofibromas
            can cause amblyopia
            difficult to remove
            can be debulked
              ± frontalis sling
            increases risk of glaucoma on the affected side
        CNS
          optic nerve gliomas
          chiasmal gliomas
            try to watch them
              if significant proptosis ||
              elevated ICP
                debulking
                ± chemo and radiation
    glaucoma
      especially in eyes with plexiform neurofibromas
    increased risk of secondary tumours
      JXG
      retinal capillary haemangioma
      rhabdomyosarcoma
      pheochromocytoma
      Wilms tumour
      leukemia
    NF1 diagnostic criteria
      2+ features
        1st degree FH
        6+ cafe au lait spots
        2+ axillary || inguinal freckles
        2+ cutaneous neurofibromas
        1+ plexiform neurofibroma
        2+ lisch nodules
        optic pathway glioma
        osseous dysplasia
          COMMON: sphenoid dysplasia
    // MRI is not necessary at baseline with good VA and a normal nerve

  NF2
    RARE
    1/2 AD
    1/2 sporadic
    neurofibromas
    acoustic neuromas
    meningioma
    schwannoma
    PSC cataracts

  sporadic tuberous sclerosis
    cognitive impairment
    seizures
    facial angiofibromas
    retinal hamartomas
      COMMON: inner-retinal glial tumours AKA astrocytic hamartoma

  AD VHL
    retinal and CNS capillary haemangioblastomas
      ALWAYS: MRI
    renal carcinoma
    pheochromocytoma
    treatment for retinal capillary haemangioblastomas
      observation
      laser
        for small lesions
      cryo
      ± antiVEGF

  sporadic Sturge-Weber syndrome AKA encephalotrigeminal angiomatosis
    CNS calcification
      ALWAYS: MRI
    seizures
    focal neurologic deficits
    facial port-wine stain
    glaucoma
      same side as the PWS
      bimodal presentation
        congenital
        later in life
      hard to control with medication
      frequently requires trab
    diffuse choroidal haemangioma

  AR ataxia telangiectasia AKA Louis-Barr syndrome
    CNS motor dysfunction
    poor saccades with head thrusting
    conjunctival telangiectasias

  XLD incontinentia pigmenti
    only in girls (boys die)
    bullous skin lesions
    hydrocephalus
    seizures
    ischaemic peripheral retina
      VH
      TRD
      NVG
    ROP-style laser management

  sporadic Wyburn-Mason syndrome
    CNS AV malformations
      ALWAYS: MRI
    retinal AV malformations
      watch them unless they bleed or cause glaucoma
